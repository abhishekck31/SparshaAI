The Sparsha Protocol

How we bridge the gap from sound to survival. Our four-step clinical handshake ensures no patient is left behind.

01

Sub-Acoustic Monitoring

Sparsha monitors ward audio at the sub-vocal level, using deep learning to identify clinical distress keywords even in high-decibel environments.

02

Cognitive Triage

Intent is processed through our Groq-accelerated LLM pipeline, identifying the specific room, patient ID, and clinical urgency in under 200ms.

03

Multi-Cast Orchestration

The alert is multi-cast via secure SSE tunnels to ward-specific pagers. Our Echo Shield prevents network loops and ensures delivery.

04

Vocal Handover

Once the responder confirms the alert, Sparsha vocally confirms the handover to the clinician, closing the response loop.

Technical FAQ

How does Sparsha handle ward noise?
Sparsha uses a proprietary "Acoustic Masking" layer that subtracts known hospital sounds (monitors, HVAC, carts) from the input stream before processing for intent.
Is patient data stored?
No. Sparsha processes all audio at the edge. We do not store recordings. Only metadata (Room Number, Incident Type) is logged for the EMR audit trail.
How does the 1km Ambulance rule work?
We integrate with ambulance GPS via our API. The system monitors proximity and only triggers the hardware siren at the 1km mark to prevent "Alarm Fatigue" in the triage bay.
Does it integrate with Epic or Cerner?
Yes. Sparsha supports FHIR and HL7 protocols for seamless integration with all major EMR providers.
